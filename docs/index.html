Some Explanations

This prototype displays an idea for an attack simulator based on the mental health experiences I have had. Due to this, as I disclaimer I should put that I am not attempting to generalize how all people deal with various mental health issues, I am just trying to display my own.

The "Medimon's" within this attack simulator are representations of the medications I take, or have taken in the past, to manage my mental health disorders. I have used some elements from the fantasy genre to portray them.

The table below details explanations for the Medimon's.

Table of Medimon's

  • Medimon Name
  • Medication Name
  • Description of Medication
  • Methylphenidragon
  • Methylphenidate
  • A stimulant, sometimes used in the treatment of ADHD, that helps to fight off fatigue and general depressive feelings.
  • Clobazore
  • Urbanol (or Clobazam)
  • A sedative, used to help cope with anxiety, induces calmness, can help while dealing with stresfull situations.
  • Seratonia Guardian
  • Selective Seratonin Reabsorbtion Inhibitor (SSRIs)
  • A medication used to prevent the reabsorbtion of seratonin in the brain, when used over a long period of time, it helps with the general management of anxiety and depression.

  • Beta Blockers
  • When used for anxiety, Beta Blockers block the release of adrenaline from entering your heart, when experiencing heavy anxiety over a situation, adrenaline can make this response much worse, and beta blockers can help manage this.

  • Self care is definitely not a great solution for mental illness, however, there are also definitely benefits to taking care of yourself. While taking daily walks or drinking tea or doing other things to manage your wellbeing won't cure mental illnesses, they can help with improving your general quality of life and getting through stresfull periods. I find that practicing self care along with steady usage of my prescribed medications definitely works well for me.

The "Brain Monster's" in this simulator are portrayals of the mental illnesses and other mental-based struggles that I personally deal with, represented in a kind of fantasy way.

The table below details the brain monsters and what they represent.

Table of Brain Monsters

Sometimes I cannot explain why exactly I experience anxiety, sometimes so bad I am brought to tears or left feeling filled with dread. Often, unfortunately, there is no reason.

Situations like this come with their own issues, but they also excacerbate existing conditions such as anxiety and depression.

As for how the attack simulator works, each Medimon and each Brain Monster come with a selection of attacks, representing various traits that they have.

The Medimon's attack strength and chance of an attack landing are dependant on the Brain Monster that is chosen. Some Medimon's are more effective against some Brain Monster's, and less effective against others.

The brain monster's attack strength is based on the specific attack used by the Medimon, sometimes the attack used will increase the strength of the brain monster's attack, and some attacks will weaken them.

After selecting attack, a summary is generated of the attack that took place. It states whether or not the chosen Medimon's attack was sucessful, the strength of the attack whether or not the attack landed, and the Brain Monster's attack strength and whether or not their attack landed.

This prototype is obviously by no means a fleshed out game at all, it is merely a simulator to demonstrate a partof the concept laid out in my hypothesis for this exam project.
